Exploring the complex nature of the reactor of our future



The elusive nuclear fusion power plant finally seems to be on the horizon. Many of the private companies that have recently surged into the market have set their sights on the 2030's to deliver electricity to the grid via nuclear fusion, with ITER's successor, DEMO, not far behind. Whilst it remains to be seen just how long it will take for fusion power plants to become a staple of our society, I firmly believe that this will happen, and is one of the next fundamental steps in the advancement of our species.

For this reason I have created a webpage to explore further the components of a tokamak, the device that is heralded to first deliver fusion energy to our society. I encourage you to browse the links on the right-hand side of this page, and immerse yourself in the workings of the tokamak, often described as 'the greatest engineering challenge that man will face in the 21st century.
