Exploring the complex nature of the reactor of our future



The elusive nuclear fusion reactor finally seems to be on the horizon. Many of the private companies that have recently surged into the market have set their sights on the 2030's to deliver electricity to the grid via nuclear fusion, with ITER's successor, DEMO, not far behind. Whilst it remains to be seen just how long it will take for fusion power plants to become a staple of our society, I firmly believe that this will happen and is one of the fundamental steps in the advancement of life on our planet.

For this reason I have created this page to explore further the components of a tokamak, the device that is heralded to first deliver commerialised fusion energy to our planet. I encourage you to browse the links on the right-hand side of this page, and immerse yourself in the workings of the tokamak, often described as one of the greatest engineering challenges that mankind will face in the 21st century. Please note that most pages presume the reader has knowledge of first-year undergraduate physics.
